About Me

Welcome to my blog! I’m Al Waleed, a passionate cybersecurity professional focused on red teaming, malware development, and Active Directory security. As a Certified Red Team Professional (CRTP), I specialize in simulating real-world attacks to identify and exploit vulnerabilities, ultimately helping organizations strengthen their security posture.

My journey in cybersecurity began with a curiosity about how systems work and how they can be secured. Through various training courses and hands-on experience, I have honed my skills in penetration testing, network security, and vulnerability assessments. This blog serves as my platform to share insights, document my learning experiences, and contribute to the ever-evolving cybersecurity community.

Here, you’ll find detailed write-ups from platforms like VulnLab, Hack The Box (HTB), and TryHackMe (THM). These write-ups reflect my progress in red teaming and aim to help others navigate the challenges of learning in this specialized field. I believe in the power of community and the importance of sharing knowledge, which is why I strive to create content that can benefit both beginners and seasoned professionals.

In addition to red teaming and malware development, I am particularly interested in the security aspects of Active Directory. Understanding its complexities and vulnerabilities is crucial for any red team professional, and I am dedicated to mastering this area.

As I continue my journey, I am committed to enhancing my experience in malware development and Active Directory security. I am also pursuing additional certifications, including CRTO, CRTO 2, CRTE, and CETP, to further deepen my expertise and skills. My ultimate goal is to contribute to innovative security solutions that protect individuals and organizations from cyber threats.

Thank you for visiting my blog! I hope you find the content engaging and informative. Feel free to connect with me—I love hearing from fellow cybersecurity enthusiasts and exchanging ideas!
